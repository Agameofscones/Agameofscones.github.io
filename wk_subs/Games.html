Commuter Bowling

Released: 2021
Download: Here
Engine: Unity 2019

Commuter Bowling was a project for a Design Media Arts upper division at UCLA. The prompt being "Take a traditional ball game, such as ping-pong, soccer, basketball, etc. Digitize and iterate it into something new." After much trial and error, and learning everything there is to know about Unity Tilemaps (and then some) my project turned into a puzzle game with a bowling theme. Rather than a puzzle game about bowling. The most mechanically complex project of mine to date. Also the source of all the neat web friendly graphics on this site.

Juice Journey

Released: 2019
Download: Pending
Source Code: Pending
Language: Processing (Java)

The player must embark on a personal JUICE JOURNEY, that is to say they need to go buy JUICE at a local JUICESTOP. Venture aboard SANDANSKI GAS-STATION, just another podunk on the long winding Orion Trail. The Caprisun Group Holding holds all. Earth has been erased, on Juice Planet remains. Relish the experience that is the codeine infused hyper monopoly.

A class exercise done at UCLA DMA with Casey Reas (instructor) in an introductory coding course. What was originally a basic exercise on programatic thinking and mode switching quickly, over the course of 3 days, grew into a point and click adventure put together from scratch utilizing almost none of Processing's native methods.

Since, at the time of coding, I had yet to learn how to properly work with arrays (or good constructor usage) 75% of the game is hardcoded. But hey, it works!

Ministry of Post Ironic Truth

Released: 2019
Download: Pending
Source Code: Pending
Language: Processing (Java)

Ministry of Post Ironic Truth or MoPIT for short is a short game heavily influenced by Papers Please. Taking a more absurdist approach, the player must censor incoming news headlines while letting a single one through. The Ministry controls the flow of ridiculous information. The economy runs on cigarettes. Things are starting to fall apart.

Final project for the same class Juice Journey was written in with Casey Reas (instructor). Keep in mind, the game only plays to the 7th day before running out of events (and crashing).
